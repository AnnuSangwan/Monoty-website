A web domain is the address where your website lives on the internet (like www.example.com). Hosting is the service that allows your website's data (files, images, etc.) to be stored and accessed on the internet. Both are essential for making a website visible to visitors.

To purchase a domain, you need to visit a domain registrar (such as GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.). Simply search for the domain name you want, and if it's available, you can register it by paying an annual fee.

SSL (Secure Socket Layer) is a security technology that encrypts data transmitted between a user and a website. It helps protect sensitive information like passwords and credit card details. Websites with SSL are marked by a padlock in the browser bar and use "https" instead of "http."
